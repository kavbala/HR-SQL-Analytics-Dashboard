# Business Summary - SQL - Based HR Employee Analytics

**File:** 04_business_summary.md
**Description:** High-level findings, key patterns, HR recoomendations, and
overall analysis.
       
## Project Overview
This analysis examines employee distibution, compensation patterns, and department-level
trends within an organization. By evaluating salary levels, bonuses, and workforce 
composition, the project aims to  support HR in understanding pay structure, identifying
performance patterns, and improving workforce planning.

----

## Key Findings
- The IT department has the highest average salary, indicating the presence of
specialized and high-value technical roles.
- Certain departments employ a larger workforce, contributing significantly to total
salary expenditure.
- Employees with longer tenure generally earn higher salaries compared to recent hires.
- A noticeable salary gap exists within departments, showing differences between
junior and senior roles.
- Performance-based bonuses are concentrated a smaller group of employees, highlighting
top performers.

----

## HR Recommendations
- Review salary structure in departments with wide pay gaps to ensure fair
compensation practices.
- Identify high-performing employees earning below department average for promotion or
salary revision.
- Monitor departments with high salary expenditure for better cost control.
- Strengthen retention strategies for experienced employees contributing significantly
to department performance.
- Use salary grades and rankings to standardize apprasial and bonus policies.

----

## Conclusion
This SQL-based HR analytics project demonstrates how structured queries and
storytelling analysis can convert raw HR data into actionable business insights.
The project showcase foundational SQL skills and analytical thinking suitable for a
junior data analytics role.
